Traffic Rules and Motor Vehicle Acts in India
This document provides a simplified educational overview of major traffic rules, road safety regulations, and Motor
Vehicle Acts applicable across India. Traffic enforcement is mainly governed by the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 and
subsequent amendments, especially the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019. Each Indian state may
additionally implement local traffic regulations, fines, permit systems, and enforcement policies.
Common Traffic Rules Across India
Rule
Description
Driving License
Valid driving license is mandatory for all motor vehicle drivers.
Helmet Rule
Helmet compulsory for rider and pillion rider on two-wheelers.
Seat Belt
Seat belt mandatory for driver and passengers.
Speed Limits
Must follow road-specific and city-specific speed limits.
Drunk Driving
Driving under alcohol influence is punishable with heavy fines and imprisonment.
Mobile Phone Usage
Using mobile phone while driving is prohibited unless hands-free.
Signal Compliance
Jumping traffic signals is punishable.
Insurance
Third-party insurance is mandatory.
Pollution Certificate
PUC certificate must be valid.
Juvenile Driving
Allowing minors to drive is punishable for guardians.
Important Penalties
Major penalties under the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019 include: Drunk driving: Fine up to ■10,000
and/or imprisonment. Driving without license: Fine up to ■5,000. No helmet: Fine and license suspension in some
states. Over-speeding: Fine varies by vehicle type. Dangerous driving: Heavy penalties and possible
imprisonment. Driving without insurance: Fine and legal action.
State-wise Traffic Enforcement Highlights
Tamil Nadu: Strict helmet enforcement, e-challan system, and road safety surveillance in Chennai and major
cities.
Karnataka: Heavy enforcement of drunk driving and signal violations using camera systems.
Maharashtra: Mumbai traffic police use automated challan systems and strict parking regulations.
Delhi: Advanced CCTV monitoring and strict pollution compliance checks.
Kerala: AI camera-based traffic monitoring and strict road safety awareness campaigns.
Telangana: Strong enforcement against triple riding and rash driving.
Andhra Pradesh: Focus on highway safety and helmet enforcement.
Gujarat: Strict implementation of commercial vehicle permits and fitness rules.
West Bengal: Urban traffic management with strong signal enforcement.
Rajasthan: Highway speed monitoring and commercial vehicle checks.
Punjab: Road safety drives and drunk driving checks.
Haryana: Strict implementation near NCR regions and highways.
Uttar Pradesh: Digital challan systems implemented in major cities.
Bihar: Increasing enforcement of helmet and license compliance.
Odisha: Road safety campaigns and digital enforcement.
Road Safety Guidelines
Road Safety Tips: Always wear helmets and seat belts. Do not use mobile phones while driving. Avoid
over-speeding. Never drink and drive. Follow lane discipline and traffic signs. Ensure vehicle documents are valid.
Conclusion
Traffic rules are designed to improve public safety and reduce accidents. Since traffic regulations and penalties
may change over time, citizens should also verify the latest updates from their respective State Transport
Departments and Traffic Police authorities.
